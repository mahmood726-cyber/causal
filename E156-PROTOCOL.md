## E156 Abstract (CURRENT BODY)

Can target trial emulation harmonize global observational disease burden with gold-standard randomized evidence? We implemented the CaMeA framework to synthesize IHME mortality data, World Bank economic covariates, and ClinicalTrials.gov anchor trials in a unified Bayesian workflow. The model uses G-computation and hierarchical spatio-temporal effects to adjust for confounders such as GDP per capita and the Universal Health Coverage index. The emulated causal effect for statin-based mortality reduction across four global regions was an odds ratio of 0.810 (95% credible interval 0.744 to 0.894), consistent with the anchor RCT signal. All estimates are certified through the TruthCert protocol, linking every regional causal estimand to its source evidence hash. This approach shows that observational IHME data can contribute robust causal insight when grounded by trial-based evidence anchors. The implementation remains limited by model dependence and by the coarse granularity of aggregate observational covariates.
