E156 Micro-Paper: Unified Causal Meta-Analysis with Target Trial Emulation (CaMeA)

1. Can target trial emulation successfully harmonize global observational disease burden with gold-standard randomized controlled trial evidence? 
2. We implemented the Causal Meta-Analysis (CaMeA) framework (Berenfeld 2025/2026) to synthesize IHME mortality data, World Bank economic covariates, and ClinicalTrials.gov anchor trials. 
3. The Bayesian model utilized G-computation and hierarchical spatio-temporal effects to adjust for confounders like GDP per capita and the Universal Health Coverage (UHC) index. 
4. The emulated causal effect (OR) for statin-based mortality reduction across four global regions was 0.810 [95% CrI: 0.744–0.894], demonstrating consistency with gold-standard anchor RCTs. 
5. All estimates are certified via the TruthCert protocol, linking every regional causal estimand to its source evidence hash for full reproducibility. 
6. This unified approach demonstrates that observational IHME data can provide robust causal insights when grounded by trial-based evidence anchors. 
7. The complete CaMeA pipeline, NumPyro-compatible PyMC model, and an interactive dashboard are available in the causal repository.
